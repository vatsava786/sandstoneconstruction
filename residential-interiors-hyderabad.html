How to Remodel Your Home to Reduce Stress


What an unsettling month we’ve had! Along with everyone else. It’s incredible how quickly our entire world has shifted, both professionally and personally. While we all have our own ways of managing during these uncertain times, the moments of stress are rapidly increasing due to various reasons such as job, education, business among many other reasons. While these reasons are mentally manipulative, you might want to prioritize those which can improve your overall mental health. The solution has been around you YES! – YOUR HOUSE INTERIORS.

That’s right—your home plays a surprisingly large role in how you feel. With the right colours, floor plans, and design elements in your house, you could be working wonders to reduce stress, anxiety, and depression. Let’s take a look at five ways you can revamp your house in order to improve your mental health.


MAKE USE OF NATURAL LIGHT

Seasonal affective disorder (SAD), which usually occurs during the fall and continues through the months, is a very real type of depression that is commonly associated with low levels of exposure to sunlight. No matter what the climate is like in the area that surrounds you, there are likely many ways an interior designer can help improve upon the amount of natural light entering your home. This might affect what a designer decides to do with windows, drapery, and even furniture. You might even find that something as simple as a window seat was all you needed to be nearer and dearer Mother Nature.

STICK WITH LIGHT AND/OR BRIGHT COLORS

Similar to how natural light can help improve one’s mental health, an interior designer might make use of certain colours to better reflect that light throughout your house. That doesn’t mean you have to exclude a particular colour preference you might have had for your room; chances are, there’s a brighter version of it that will suit your room perfectly. What’s more, brighter walls are also thought to create the illusion of spaciousness, which can combat any feelings of confinement you may have been experiencing.


CREATE MORE SPACE

Despite a gorgeous touch-up to your walls, you might feel that a room is particularly cluttered. This is only natural, given that we, as humans, accumulate numerous possessions over the years, and many are initially deemed too important to throw away. When you decide that a room is in need of a makeover, take it upon yourself to assess whether or not each item is worth holding onto. In the wise words of wise men, “If it doesn’t spark joy, donate it or throw it away.” The act of cleaning up and organizing your room should be enough to create a huge sense of relief; however, if you’d like to do more, ask your interior designer about the life-changing magic of modifying or opening up your floor plan to give yourself more flexibility.

HIRE AN INTERIOR DESIGNER

Naturally, the idea of tearing apart your house, room by room, without a clear vision in mind can be stressful in and of itself. Couple this with stressors you undoubtedly have from your day-to-day life and you might consider forgoing making improvements to your house altogether. But fear not: much of your stress can be whisked away by enlisting the help of an interior designer who can provide professional guidance throughout the process of giving your house a much-needed makeover. If, when taking on home improvement projects, it becomes apparent that the financial impact is becoming a source of stress, take care not to rush. By taking on projects a room at a time, it can be easier to afford and adapt to changes around the house.
